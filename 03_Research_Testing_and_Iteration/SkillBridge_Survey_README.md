# SkillBridge Survey Methodology

This document provides an overview of how the three SkillBridge research surveys were conducted, who they targeted, and what they aimed to find out.

---

## 1. Student Survey
**File:** `Skillbridge_Student_Survey_1-32.xlsx`
**Responses:** 32

### Purpose
To understand university students' attitudes toward freelance and project-based work, and to gauge interest in a platform like SkillBridge as a way to earn income and build experience during their studies.

### Distribution Method
The survey was distributed digitally through university group chats and shared directly with students the team had existing contact with. Responses were collected via Microsoft Forms.

### Target Audience
University students across a range of year groups (1st year through postgraduate) and degree areas, including STEM, Business, Humanities, Engineering, and Creative disciplines.

### Key Topics Covered
- Current employment and freelance experience
- Barriers to entering freelance or project-based work
- Availability and preferred hours per week
- Interest level in the SkillBridge concept
- Preferred payment ranges and project types
- Willingness to pay for premium features

---

## 2. SME Survey
**File:** `Skillbridge_SME_Survey_1-13.xlsx`
**Responses:** 13

### Purpose
To assess whether small and medium-sized businesses (SMEs) would consider using a platform like SkillBridge to outsource short-term projects to students, and to understand their hiring habits, concerns, and budget expectations.

### Distribution Method
The team conducted in-person outreach in Bath, approaching local SMEs directly and asking them to scan a QR code to access and complete the survey form. Responses were collected via Microsoft Forms.

### Target Audience
Local SMEs in Bath, primarily small businesses with 1–20 employees across sectors including Retail/Hospitality, Marketing/Creative, and Tech/Digital.

### Key Topics Covered
- Business size and industry sector
- Prior experience outsourcing or hiring freelancers
- Challenges faced in the hiring process
- Types of projects they would consider outsourcing
- Interest level in the SkillBridge concept
- Budget expectations and attitudes toward commission/subscription pricing

---

## 3. User Testing Survey
**File:** `SkillBridge_User_Testing_1-3.xlsx`
**Responses:** 3

### Purpose
To gather usability feedback from real students who tested the SkillBridge app prototype, using a structured System Usability Scale (SUS) questionnaire alongside open-ended questions about their experience.

### Distribution Method
Students who the team had existing contact with were invited to use the SkillBridge app and then complete a feedback form. Responses were collected via Microsoft Forms.

### Target Audience
Students already familiar with the SkillBridge project who could test the app and provide informed, detailed feedback.

### Key Topics Covered
- Specific pain points or moments of confusion during use
- What felt intuitive or easy to navigate
- Likelihood of real-world use
- Suggestions for improvement
- Willingness to pay for premium features (student and university sides)
- Acceptability of platform fees for SMEs

---

## Notes
- All three surveys were hosted on Microsoft Forms.
- All responses were anonymous; participants confirmed they were 18 or older and consented to participate before completing the survey.
- Survey links and result overviews are available in `results_overview.txt`.
